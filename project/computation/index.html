Computational Geophysics

Mar 27, 2014
Slides Follow

Extracting important and relevant physics from data and developing models with predictive power will be a significant task in the future of Earth sciences. Many of my observation-oriented projects share the theme of utilizing a probabilistic inference framework (analytical and numerical Bayesian approaches) with an emphasis on characterizing uncertainty in data and modeling, and propagating that uncertainty to inferred/predicted parameters. The other part of my research involves developing computational models with sophisticated physics and predictive powers to understand earthquakes, tsunamis, or slow slip. I take a special interest in the synergy of developing inverse and forward modeling in geophysical problems to eventually improve observation-drive, physics-based inference about the future behavior of our Earth.
