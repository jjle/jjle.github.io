School of Geosciences

Mewbourne College of Earth and Energy

University of Oklahoma

Hi! I am a geophysicist, born and raised in China and trained in Southern California. I am fascinated by the dynamic processes of the Earth and strive to understand their underlying mechanisms and predictability, with the ultimate goals to improve the assessment and migitation of geo-hazards and safe, sustainable exploration of geo-energy.

My research is focused on Earth’s deformation processes due to faulting and human activities over timescales spanning seconds to centuries, through a combination of geophysical observational techniques, primiarly Global Positioning System (GPS) and Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR), seismicity observations, and laboratory-based mechanical models. I am also passionate about uncertainty quantification in geophysical research. Many of the problems I study are interdisciplinary, requiring knowledge from and collaborations across seismology, geodesy, geology, rock mechanics, computational science, civil and environmental engineering, etc.

I started as an Assistant Professor of Geophysics in the School of Geosciences at the University of Oklahoma in Fall 2020. I am looking to recruit several MS/PhD students and a postdoc to join the team. Please contact me for research opportunities if you are interested!
